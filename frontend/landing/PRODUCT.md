# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Working scientists, ML engineers, and computational researchers evaluating an
open-source research agent. They arrive skeptical of hype, fluent in the
terminal, and looking for one thing: is this real, and can I read the source?
The page has to earn a `curl | bash` or a GitHub star in one scroll.

## Product Purpose

The landing page for **OpenScience** (openscience.sh) — the open-source release
of the SynSci CLI (`synsci`). It runs the whole research loop (read, hypothesize,
implement, run, record) in the browser or the terminal, wires into ~30 scientific
databases, and ships 17 domains of built-in skills. Free, AGPL-3.0, no account.
Success = installs and GitHub stars from researchers who trust it because they
can see exactly what it does.

## Brand Personality

Open, rigorous, grounded. The voice of a careful scientist, not a startup pitch.
It shows real commands, real citations, real reproducible results instead of
claiming capability. Warm paper-and-ink, not cold SaaS blue. Confident without
shouting; every claim is something you could go verify in the source.

## Anti-references

- SaaS-cream / purple-gradient-on-white landing pages (the 2026 AI default).
- "AI scientist" hype framing, "supercharge/seamless/transform" copy, badge-pill
  rows ("Open source · No account · Fast").
- Green "phosphor" terminal costume; widget-bento dashboards with blinking lights.
- Framing as "an alternative to X"; naming specific model vendors.
- Anything that blinks or pulses. The user finds it enraging.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the real work.** Real terminal sessions, real database names, real
   citations and ΔΔG values. Never a mock that couldn't actually run.
2. **Open by default.** The pitch is legibility: read it, fork it, write your own.
   The design should feel as inspectable as the source.
3. **Calm confidence.** No hype, no urgency theater. Restraint reads as trust.
4. **Warm-technical, not cold-SaaS.** Paper/ink warmth carried by the serif and
   the coral accent; the terminal supplies the technical register.
5. **Fewer, better surfaces.** Boxes are a last resort. Let typography, imagery,
   and whitespace carry the page; reserve framed panels for real product shots.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast on all body copy. `prefers-reduced-motion` fully honored (reveals
and the terminal collapse to static). Nothing blinks or auto-plays audio. Focus
states visible. Semantic landmarks and aria-labels on icon-only controls.
